The Bluenumi Clock

The Bluenumi Clock is a small, functional, and beautiful desktop clock featuring the warm "retro" glow of 1970s-era numitron display technology from the former Soviet Union.

Designed for makers and tinkerers who want an easy-to-assemble, 100% open-source kit clock that has nixie tube aesthetics, Bluenumi uses only through-hole electronic components and low voltages. It's also hackable. Programmed with Arduino-compatible firmware licensed under the GPL, experienced Arduino hackers can customize and reprogram the Bluenumi firmware using the standard Arduino IDE and a TTL serial cable or adapter.

Features

  • "Retro" incandescent glowing display, featuring Soviet IV-9 numitron tubes
  • DS1307 real time clock with backup battery for accurate timekeeping and alarm memory preservation during loss of power
  • Blue LED underlighting for each digit, with customizable fade patterns
  • Programmable alarm
  • 12- or 24-hour operating mode with AM/PM indicator light
  • Easy display blanking for nighttime use and tube life preservation
  • 100% open-source hardware and software
  • Arduino programming compatibility using standard TTL serial (adapter not included)

Assembly

The kit includes all electronic components, battery, a well-marked PCB, switch mode power supply (US 120V/60Hz), acrylic case and associated hardware. You supply a temperature-controlled soldering iron, solder (leaded or lead-free), phillips screwdriver, multimeter, and two to three hours of spare time.
